## Shared Trait: AI-Aware Non-Builders

Both ICPs share a critical characteristic - they are **not beginners**. They:

- **Know AI has changed the game** - aware that custom software is now fast and cheap
- **Have tried building themselves** - used Lovable, Bolt, Cursor, or at least researched them
- **Hit a skill ceiling, not an awareness ceiling** - they know what's possible, they just can't execute
- **Need an execution partner, not an educator** - don't need to be sold on AI's potential

### Why This Matters

A year ago, building a custom dashboard to unify 10 APIs would cost months and a team of 5-10 devs. People wouldn't even consider it. Now they know it's feasible - they've seen demos, tried prototypes, read the content.

The unlock has happened. They just need someone to finish the job.

## Content Implications

### What the content should NOT do:
- Explain what AI can do (they already know)
- Sell the "AI revolution" narrative (they're already bought in)
- Treat them as beginners needing education
- Use generic "transform your business with AI" messaging

### What the content SHOULD do:
- Speak to the gap between prototype and production
- Acknowledge they've tried (validates their effort)
- Position P0STMAN as the execution layer, not the education layer
- Use their vocabulary: Lovable, Bolt, Cursor, vibe coding, prototypes, ceiling
- Be peer-to-peer with someone further along the skill curve

### Tone
Not guru-to-novice. More like: "You've seen what's possible. You've tried. Now let's actually ship it."
