Our A-D Evidence Grading System

How we evaluate and rank health products

A

Strong Evidence

Requirements:

  • Multiple high-quality randomized controlled trials (RCTs)
  • Consistent results across studies
  • Large sample sizes (>500 participants total)
  • Low risk of bias in study design
  • Clinically significant effect sizes

Examples:

Creatine for strength training, Vitamin D for bone health

B

Good Evidence

Requirements:

  • Several well-designed studies with positive results
  • Generally consistent findings
  • Moderate sample sizes (100-500 participants)
  • Some limitations but overall solid methodology
  • Meaningful but modest effect sizes

Examples:

Magnesium for sleep, Omega-3 for heart health

C

Limited Evidence

Requirements:

  • Mixed or inconsistent study results
  • Small or poorly designed studies
  • Limited participant numbers (<100)
  • Methodological concerns or high bias risk
  • Conflicting evidence from different research groups

Examples:

Black cohosh for menopause, many herbal supplements

D

Poor Evidence

Requirements:

  • Predominantly negative or null results
  • Very poor study quality or design flaws
  • Evidence of harm or safety concerns
  • Marketing claims not supported by research
  • Lack of peer-reviewed scientific studies

Examples:

Detox teas, most "fat burner" supplements

Our Research Process

The systematic approach behind every product evaluation

1

Literature Review

Comprehensive search of peer-reviewed databases (PubMed, Cochrane, etc.) for all relevant studies on the ingredient or product category.

2

Study Quality Assessment

Each study is evaluated for methodology, sample size, bias risk, and relevance to real-world use cases.

3

Evidence Synthesis

Results are analyzed for consistency, effect sizes, and clinical significance. Conflicting evidence is transparently noted.

4

Product Evaluation

Individual products are assessed for ingredient quality, dosing accuracy, third-party testing, and manufacturing standards.

5

Community Integration

Real user outcomes are collected through structured reporting and integrated with scientific evidence for complete picture.
