Glide Limitations: What It Can't Do

Glide Apps is build mobile and web apps from google sheets or airtable without coding. While it excels at internal tools, simple mobile apps, prototypes, it has significant limitations for customer-facing apps, complex data, high-scale applications. The main issues are: limited by spreadsheet architecture, limited customization, not for production apps.

Key Takeaway: Glide gets you about 70% of the way to a working app. The last 30% typically requires professional development to achieve production quality.

Main Limitations of Glide

Limited by Spreadsheet Architecture

Using Google Sheets as a database works for simple apps but breaks down with complex data relationships or scale.

Limited Customization

Glide's templates and components are limiting. Custom designs and features require workarounds.

Not for Production Apps

Glide is great for prototypes and internal tools but not robust enough for customer-facing production apps.

Performance with Large Datasets

Apps slow down significantly as Google Sheets grows beyond a few thousand rows.

When Glide Works Well

Glide is best for: Internal tools, simple mobile apps, prototypes

  • Very fast to get started
  • Google Sheets as database
  • Good for simple apps
  • Mobile-first design

When to Avoid Glide

Glide is NOT suitable for: Customer-facing apps, complex data, high-scale applications

  • Spreadsheet database limits
  • Slow with large datasets
  • Limited design flexibility
  • Not production-ready

Frequently Asked Questions

Can Glide handle thousands of users?

Not well. Glide uses Google Sheets as a database, which isn't designed for concurrent users. Beyond a few hundred users or a few thousand rows, performance degrades significantly.

Is Glide good for a startup MVP?

For very simple apps, yes. For anything with complex data or expecting real users, you'll quickly hit limits. Consider it for internal tools or very early prototyping only.
